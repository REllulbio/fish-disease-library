The term pasteurellosis has previously been wrongly associated with disease caused by *Photobacterium* *damselae* subsp. *piscicida* (family Vibrionaceae) (formerly incorrectly classified as *Pasteurella* *piscicida*) [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full). Correctly used, pasteurellosis is defined as the disease caused by *bona fide* species of the Pasteurellaceae family.

## Symptoms of Pasteurellosis

### Common Symptoms

Atlantic Salmon: 

- **Physical Signs:**
    - **Septicemia:** Systemic bacterial infection affecting multiple organs. [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).
    - **Hemorrhage and inflammation:** Ulcers in skeletal and cardiac muscle, hemorrhaging in internal organs, and inflammation around the pericardium, abdominal wall, pseudobranchs, and the base of the pectoral fins [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).
    - **Exophthalmia:**  Severe inflammation of the eye and eye socket in some cases [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).
    - **Histopathology**: Abundant inflammatory cells, tissue necrosis, and short rod-shaped bacteria in affected organs [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).

- **Behavioral Changes:**
    - **Lethargy:** Reduced activity and sluggish swimming  [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).
    - **Loss of Appetite:** Decreased feeding behavior [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).
    - **Abnormal Swimming Patterns:** Fish may swim sluggishly and in random patterns [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).

**Progression of Symptoms**

- **Early Stages:** Subtle signs such as reduced feed intake and slight lethargy.

- **Advanced Stages:** While the disease can become clinically obvious late in the production cycle, outbreaks have also occurred in newly transferred salmon, and disease progression may lead to severe pathology and/or early harvest decisions because of poor welfare [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish), [7](https://www.microbiologyresearch.org/content/journal/ijsem/10.1099/ijsem.0.006729#tab2). 

- **Impact on Fish Health:** Pasteurellosis severely compromises overall health and often leads to significant mortality [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway), [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381). Research has shown that mortalities resulting directly from outbreaks ranges from low to moderate [7](https://doi.org/10.1111/jfd.13381), [9](https://doi.org/10.1111/jfd.14085).

Lumpfish: 

- **Physical Signs:**
    - **Septicemia:** Systemic bacterial infection affecting multiple organs [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg),  [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381), [11](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1050464819310587).
    - **Hemorrhages:** Reddened areas around the jaw and base of fins [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg),  [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381), [11](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1050464819310587).
    - **Histopathology:** Systemic infection with bacterial aggregates observed in tissue sections of internal organs [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg), [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).

**Progression of Symptoms**

- **Acute:** Skin lesions, haemorrhage at fin bases [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg), [11](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1050464819310587).

- **Chronic:** Characteristic white spots on skin/around eyes, frayed fins, redness around mouth/fin bases [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg), [11](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1050464819310587?casa_token=uYme_FZ-c8YAAAAA:Uj2PMDz9OKLezgXQrOAml6ZwaAtryDO02cCfvtfYFuHFsvoXF1dCU2LfkQoG1mgKlqGkmxve).

- **Impact on Fish Health:** Up to 100% mortality observed in infected lumpfish, with survivors potentially acting as asymptomatic carriers of the bacteria that can lead to re-emergence of disease if triggered by a stress event [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg).  Compared to salmon, lumpfish seem highly susceptible and may develop marked pathology even when salmon exposed to similar isolates show few or no clinical signs [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381).

### Causes of Pasteurellosis

**Etiology**

- **Causative Agent:** Bacteria in the genus *Phocoenobacter*, namely the species *skyensis*, and *atlanticus* which includes the subspecies *atlanticus* and *cyclopteri* [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full), [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg).

- **Transmission Methods:**
    - **Horizontal transmission:** While exact transmission routes and survival strategies in seawater are still unknown for the bacteria, outbreaks recorded in nearby locations within the same time period can indicate horizontal transmission occurs between farms [10](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2026/fiskehelserapporten-2025/_/attachment/inline/363a7595-e0c3-4ee8-87e6-71ba808b4288:b3c8e9bf1ee7902bdc1ab93334e5219666ceabbd/Fiskehelserapporten%202025.pdf). Under laboratory conditions, transmission of disease from lumpfish to Atlantic salmon in a cohabitation environment was unsuccessful, however lumpfish are highly susceptible to disease caused by both subspecies [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381).
    - Pasteurellosis occurs at all lumpfish life stages, and the bacteria have been detected in eggs and milt, suggesting possible vertical transmission [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg).

**Risk Factors**

- **Farm Management Practices:** Stress conditions, such as delousing treatments, can increase susceptibility to pasteurellosis. Outbreaks often reported 2–3 weeks after handling such as lice treatment [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381), [12](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0347930), [9](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.14085).
- Strong statistical association between thermal (OR≈2.5) and mechanical (OR≈1.8) delousing and *Ph. atlanticus* detection; freshwater delousing not associated with increased risk [9](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.14085).
- Increased salmon weight and previous farm‑cycle *Ph. atlanticus* diagnosis increase odds of new detection [9](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.14085).

### Diagnosis

**Diagnostic Methods**

- **Clinical Examination:** Observation of physical and behavioral symptoms [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway), [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).

- **Laboratory Tests:**
    - **Bacterial Culture:** Isolation and identification of *Phocoenobacter atlanticus* on blood agar with 2% NaCl. Grows as small grey colonies within 2-4 days, with slight alpha hemolysis [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish). 
    - **MALDI-TOF MS:** Mass spectrometry for rapid identification [6](https://www.vetinst.no/en/diseases/pasteurellosis-in-fish).
    - **Whole Genome Sequencing:** For precise identification and discrimination of *Phocoenobacter atlanticus* isolates [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full), [7](https://www.microbiologyresearch.org/content/journal/ijsem/10.1099/ijsem.0.006729#tab2).

**Differential Diagnosis**

- **Distinguishing pasteurellosis from other diseases:** It is crucial to differentiate pasteurellosis from other bacterial diseases with similar symptoms, such as atypical furunculosis (in lumpfish) and typical furunculosis (in Atlantic salmon). Biochemical and molecular testing of the bacteria can be used to distinguish between the two diseases.

### Prophylaxis, treatment and control

**Vaccination- lumpfish:**

- Experimental monovalent vaccines against pasteurellosis induce high specific antibody levels but only **partial protection**, with high mortality still observed and no reduction in bacterial load in survivors [11](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1050464819310587).
- Bath challenge and immunology work suggest humoral antibodies alone are insufficient; other immune components are likely important [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg).
- Current commercial lumpfish vaccines do not include *Phocoenobacter atlanticus* subsp. *cyclopteri* antigens; vaccine development is still in early stages [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg), [13](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-2607/9/6/1215). 

**Vaccination- Atlantic salmon:**

- While Atlantic salmon are typically vaccinated against many bacterial diseases, no commercial vaccine against pasteurellosis is available. Autogenous vaccines are in use, however their efficacy is not well documented [10](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2026/fiskehelserapporten-2025/_/attachment/inline/363a7595-e0c3-4ee8-87e6-71ba808b4288:b3c8e9bf1ee7902bdc1ab93334e5219666ceabbd/Fiskehelserapporten%202025.pdf), [14](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2025/fiskehelserapporten-2024/_/attachment/inline/a9141dc4-f516-47be-9261-5efe946bc579:54f6073452f4b7923f3355f3afc8e11308075a28/Fiskehelserapporten%202024_Utgave%202.pdf). 

**Antibacterial therapy- lumpfish:**

- In experimental infections, medicated feed with florfenicol 20 mg/kg/day gave good early effect against pasteurellosis, improving survival, while oxolinic acid and flumequine were not effective; protection waned later in the trial [15](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jfd.13545).
- Effective protocols are important to reduce mortality and resistance risk; this is the first protocol‑oriented study in lumpfish [15](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jfd.13545).
- For hatcheries, antibiotics are used but no approved standardized protocols exist; treatment in sea cages is difficult because of cohabiting salmon [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg). Furthermore, oral administration via medicated feed is often ineffective because dying fish typically stop eating [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).

**Preventive Measures**

- **Biosecurity Protocols:**
    - Implementing strict biosecurity measures on farms and well boats to prevent the introduction and spread of disease.
    - Proper cleaning and disinfection of transport vessels and well boats between treatments and localities [16](https://www.tekna.no/fag-og-nettverk/miljo-og-biovitenskap/tekna-havbruk/retningslinjer-for-hygienekontroll-av-fartoy-innen-akvakultur/#forord).
    - Regular monitoring through the use of eDNA [12]([Strand et al., 2026](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0347930), and rapid diagnosis of suspected cases [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).

- **Farm Management Practices:**
    - Careful consideration of cleaner fish use and potential disease transmission [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381).
    - The association with thermal/mechanical delousing suggests that shifting toward freshwater delousing could reduce salmon pasteurellosis risk [9](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.14085).
    - Pasteurellosis has been recorded soon after transfer of lumpfish to salmon cages and following stress events, emphasizing stress minimization [3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12905?casa_token=Adn8S3CfvnsAAAAA:ihryvVUH-Bm88XthU5hHD9xr3BC8EHh7qqPZNG6PHBU4nlVzwG1oijptimWgvllXKnbKFCDZdse3Jg).

### Case Studies

**Real-World Examples**

- **Notable Outbreaks:**
    - **Norway (2012):** First documentation of pasteurellosis in lumpfish, caused by *Ph. atlanticus* subsp. *cyclopteri* [2](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jfd.12366?casa_token=B_EZwK3lfp4AAAAA%3ACmW4N_RL8PDRkw8zBtjFzgTdBFtIIucaDXJbJJNvHj1GFoDoygn_gl5Df524Z-npFrWSnFputirfyA).
    - **Norway (2018):** First documentation of concerning number of outbreaks of pasteurellosis in Atlantic salmon caused by *Ph. atlanticus* subsp. *atlanticus*
    - **Norway (2020-2022):** Approximately 50 diagnosed cases annually in south-western Norway [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full). Also detected as far north as PO10 in 2024 and PO7 in 2025  [10](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2026/fiskehelserapporten-2025/_/attachment/inline/363a7595-e0c3-4ee8-87e6-71ba808b4288:b3c8e9bf1ee7902bdc1ab93334e5219666ceabbd/Fiskehelserapporten%202025.pdf), [14](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2025/fiskehelserapporten-2024/_/attachment/inline/a9141dc4-f516-47be-9261-5efe946bc579:54f6073452f4b7923f3355f3afc8e11308075a28/Fiskehelserapporten%202024_Utgave%202.pdf).
    - **Scotland:** Outbreaks typically associated with *Phocoenobacter skyensis* serotype O2 [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).

- **Response Strategies and Outcomes:**
    - **Norway:** Increased focus on rapid diagnosis and identification of *Ph. atlanticus* isolates [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full), [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).
    - **Norway**: The Food Safety Authority is considering listing pasteurellosis in Atlantic salmon a notifiable disease [17](https://hoering.mattilsynet.no/hoering/3823).
    - **Scotland:** Development of serotyping methods for *Ph. skyensis* [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway).

- **Lessons Learned:** The importance of routine bacteriology, rapid diagnosis, stress reduction, and proper biosecurity measures in controlling pasteurellosis outbreaks [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full), [5](https://eafpbulletin.scholasticahq.com/article/31534-pasteurella-skyensis-in-atlantic-salmon-salmo-salar-l-in-western-norway), [16](https://www.tekna.no/fag-og-nettverk/miljo-og-biovitenskap/tekna-havbruk/retningslinjer-for-hygienekontroll-av-fartoy-innen-akvakultur/#forord).

    - Recent outbreaks have been primarily associated with Western Norway, with new outbreaks being recorded further north (PO10 and PO7) in 2024 and 2025 [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full), [10](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2026/fiskehelserapporten-2025/_/attachment/inline/363a7595-e0c3-4ee8-87e6-71ba808b4288:b3c8e9bf1ee7902bdc1ab93334e5219666ceabbd/Fiskehelserapporten%202025.pdf), [14](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2025/fiskehelserapporten-2024/_/attachment/inline/a9141dc4-f516-47be-9261-5efe946bc579:54f6073452f4b7923f3355f3afc8e11308075a28/Fiskehelserapporten%202024_Utgave%202.pdf).

- **Economic Impact:**
    - While specific economic figures are not available, the increasing frequency of outbreaks suggests a growing economic concern for the salmon farming industry in Norway [1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2023.1236290/full).

- **Treatment & Management:**
    - There is currently no commercially available vaccine against pasteurellosis in lumpsfish or Atlantic salmon, however autogenous vaccines are available for Atlantic salmon [10](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2026/fiskehelserapporten-2025/_/attachment/inline/363a7595-e0c3-4ee8-87e6-71ba808b4288:b3c8e9bf1ee7902bdc1ab93334e5219666ceabbd/Fiskehelserapporten%202025.pdf), [14](https://www.vetinst.no/rapporter-og-publikasjoner/rapporter/2025/fiskehelserapporten-2024/_/attachment/inline/a9141dc4-f516-47be-9261-5efe946bc579:54f6073452f4b7923f3355f3afc8e11308075a28/Fiskehelserapporten%202024_Utgave%202.pdf). 
    - Research is ongoing to establish factors leading to disease outbreaks and how to best experimentally challenge fish with this emerging pathogen, as well as research into vaccine development [8](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/jfd.13381), [13](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-2607/9/6/1215), [19](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2026.1793695/full).
#### Scotland

- **Pasteurellosis incidence in Scotland:**
    - *Ph. skyensis* was first discovered in Scotland at three separate sites over a four-year period [4](https://www.microbiologyresearch.org/content/journal/ijsem/10.1099/00207713-52-3-699).
    - Recent outbreaks in Scotland have been associated with serotype O2 of *Ph. skyensis* [20](https://eafp.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/2019-porto-19-eafp-abstract-book.pdf). 

- **Geographical spread:**
    - Published literature lists outbreaks occurring in localities on the Isle of Skye and in the Western Highlands and Outer Hebrides of Scotland [20](https://eafp.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/2019-porto-19-eafp-abstract-book.pdf).

- **Economic impact:**
    - While specific economic impact data for Scotland is available, one case study mentions 500 tonnes of Atlantic salmon with an average weight of 4kg were lost during a single outbreak at a seawater site on the Isle of Lewis [20](https://eafp.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/2019-porto-19-eafp-abstract-book.pdf).

- **Treatment & management:**
    - Similar to Norway, there is no mention of a commercially available vaccine against pasteurellosis in Scotland.

## Conclusion

Pasteurellosis in Atlantic salmon and lumpfish is an endemic, systemic bacterial disease driven mainly by *Phocoenobacter* *atlanticus* (in Norway) and *Ph. skyensis* (in Scotland). Lumpfish of all life stages are highly susceptible to *Ph. atlanticus*, develop acute and chronic systemic disease, and can act as asymptomatic carriers; salmon are susceptible to *Ph. atlanticus* subsp. *atlanticus* and *Ph. skyensis*, and tend to develop disease under farm conditions where stress and handling are present. Key risk factors include stressful delousing methods and prior farm infection history. Diagnosis relies on bacterial culture, qPCR and histopathology, but differentiation from other bacterial diseases is important. For lumpfish, florfenicol can improve survival experimentally, and vaccine efforts are underway. In salmon, there are no commercial vaccines reported but autogenous vaccines are partially protective. Management strategies, especially the choice of non-medicinal delousing methods, stress reduction and biosecurity around fish transport, are central to control while research continues to clarify reservoirs, virulence mechanisms and effective immunoprophylaxis.

This comprehensive guide aims to provide the necessary information and resources to effectively manage pasteurellosis and support the health and welfare of farmed fish.
